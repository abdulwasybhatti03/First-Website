Chicken Korma Recipe

Description :

Chicken Korma is a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced Indian curry featuring tender chicken simmered in a yogurt- and nut-based sauce infused with aromatic spices.
Chicken Korma is a classic South Asian dish with roots in Mughal cuisine, traditionally considered a royal delicacy due to its luxurious ingredients and delicate flavors .
The dish typically uses boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, marinated in a mixture of yogurt, ginger, garlic, turmeric, chili powder, and garam masala to infuse flavor before cooking

Ingredients:

  • Chicken (500 g)
  • Yogurt (1 cup)
  • Onion, finely sliced
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Ground turmeric
  • Garam masala
  • Ground coriander
  • Almonds or cashew paste
  • Oil or ghee
  • Salt, to taste

    Steps to make Chicken Korma :

  1. Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, and salt; marinate the chicken for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan and sauté sliced onions until golden brown.
  3. Add the marinated chicken and sear until lightly browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in ground coriander, garam masala, and ground turmeric; cook for 2–3 minutes to bloom the spices.
  5. Add almond or cashew paste and a little water to create a creamy sauce; bring to a gentle simmer.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat for 15–20 minutes (or until chicken is cooked through).
  7. Uncover, adjust seasoning with salt, and simmer briefly to thicken the gravy to desired consistency.
  8. Garnish with chopped cilantro and a drizzle of cream (optional), then serve hot with rice or naan.
  9. Heat oil or ghee in a pan and add the marinated chicken.
  10. Sauté the chicken until it is cooked through and slightly browned.
  11. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they are translucent.
  12. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  13. Add the ground turmeric, garam masala, and coriander.
  14. Pour in the yogurt mixture and stir well.
  15. Simmer the korma on low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  16. Blend the almonds or cashews into a paste and add to the korma.
  17. Season with salt to taste and serve hot with rice or naan.
