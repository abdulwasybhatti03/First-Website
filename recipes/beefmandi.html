Beef Mandi Recipe

Description :

Beef Mandi is a traditional Yemeni dish that has gained popularity across the Middle East.
It consists of tender, slow-cooked beef served over fragrant rice infused with a blend of spices such as cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon.
The beef is typically marinated with a mixture of yogurt and spices before being cooked in a tandoor or oven, allowing it to absorb the rich flavors.
The dish is often garnished with fried onions, nuts, and raisins, adding texture and sweetness to the savory meal.
Beef Mandi is not only a feast for the taste buds but also a cultural experience, reflecting the culinary heritage of Yemen and its neighboring regions.

Ingredients:

  • Beef (500 g)
  • Rice(1kg)
  • Yogurt (1 cup)
  • Onion, finely sliced
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Ground turmeric
  • Garam masala
  • Ground coriander
  • Almonds or cashew paste
  • Oil or ghee
  • Salt, to taste

Steps to make Beef Mandi :

  1. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes and then drain.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan and add the marinated beef.
  3. Sauté the beef until it is cooked through and slightly browned.
  4. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they are translucent.
  5. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  6. Add the ground turmeric, garam masala, and coriander.
  7. Pour in the yogurt mixture and stir well.
  8. Simmer the dish on low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Blend the almonds or cashews into a paste and add to the dish.
  10. Season with salt to taste and serve hot with rice or naan.
